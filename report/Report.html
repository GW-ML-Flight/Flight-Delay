In this section, it’s important that you clearly define how you measure success, and exactly how you do the evaluation.

  • We wanted to model the Wang paper (Wang 2026) but with DCA as the focus. Goal was to get similar results as they did with BOS for DCA. Looking for things like ROC-AUC, F1-score, and accuracy.
  • We also wanted to try to improve upon that model with additional features. This included more weather data, more derived data, and more historical data.
  • We also tried to do more of a regression approach to predict flight delay amounts, but that approach was less successful than a classification approach. Predicting actual delay times was more challenging.

5 Lessons Learned

If you think there are high level lessons (not specific things like one key trick in training process), share them here. This can be technical or operational, e.g., ideal frequency and format of group meetings in a collaborative project.

  • Homebrew is quite difficult to work with, especially on Apple Silicon. We spent a good amount of time troubleshooting XGBoost issues incorrectly stating we were running 32-bit Python on a 64-bit OS. Ended up being able to resolve the issue by ensuring the correct version of Homebrew was installed for the architecture and installing libopm.
